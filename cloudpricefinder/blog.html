“IaaS”, “SaaS” or “PaaS”? and why “aaS”?

Just think about what you need to accomplish a project as simple as a public Website on your own. First you need to have a web server that can provide your web page to those who want to access it; to do this you must have a computer that is always connected to the Internet. This means you have to buy a computer, pay for electricity, have an internet connection with a fixed public IP address, so you have to pay for it. And if you want your website to run 24/7, you also have to take maintenance into account.

Now, what happens if you start getting a lot of visits and need more power from your computer and more speed from your Internet line? In other words, how can you scale your infrastructure to meet the required performance? This is what all those who wanted to approach the Web as actors, whether individuals or small or large companies had to face.

The cloud helps to start providing a service without incurring startup costs that were affecting development resources. In cloud computing, the cloud service provider owns, manages and maintains the resources (computational, storage, Internet access); the customer consumes them through an Internet connection and pays for them on a subscription or pay-as-you-go basis. Thus, cloud solutions "as a service" offer primarily an economic benefit. You can access and scale the IT resources you need at a predictable cost, without the expense and overhead of on-premise purchase and maintenance.

Currently IaaS, PaaS and SaaS are the three most popular types of cloud service offerings. But what do these acronyms mean? IaaS, or infrastructure as a service, is on-demand access to cloud-hosted physical and virtual servers, storage and networking. You can set your own server in the cloud, for full control on your platform. PaaS, or platform as a service, is on-demand access to a complete, ready-to-use, cloud-hosted platform for developing, running, maintaining and managing applications. Never heard of Wordpress? You can use such a service to easily deploy web content. SaaS, or software as a service, is on-demand access to ready-to-use, cloud-hosted application software. Think about your personal email. Do you own a mail server or have you subscribed to a web service, such as gmail or hotmail? Now you know what the main 'as a service' solutions of the cloud vendors are.
